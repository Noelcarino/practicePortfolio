About

8/26 - Dev Story When I was first introduced to programming, I knew immediately that this was something I wanted to do for the rest of my life. Initially, I declared myself a civil engineering major, which to this day, I don’t know exactly what being a civil engineer is. The program I checked out was an hour long session that showed us how to build a flappy bird game using ‘code puzzles’ where we plug it into to other codes to see what the program executes. It was at that moment after putting together the puzzles that I realized, ‘holy shit, if I can do this with flappy bird, can’t I do this with other games that I’ve always wanted to make?” From this day forward, I made it my mission, my dream to create these ideas that have come across my mind when I thought building it was impossible. Being introduced to programming bridged that gap from impossible to a taste of a possible soon to become a reality.
That was five years ago. Since then, I have changed majors, taken some CS classes, dropped out of school and decided to pursue my other dream of becoming one of the top badminton players in America. I was working for McDonalds at the time I decided to become a badminton coach at Hana Badminton Academy, where I got the opportunity to work with a former Korean National player, former Chinese province team player, 2016 Olympians, and a former Target manager who oversaw all the targets of the Los Angeles region. Because of them, I was able to represent U.S.A at the Pan-American Badminton Team Championships Despite pursuing one of my dreams, I knew in the back of my mind that being a badminton athlete wasn’t going to last forever. I have learned other coding languages during my free time and did my best to make “pretty cool” websites that only function with HTML and CSS. I thought I was the shit when I putting up HTML and CSS elements together to form a “well developed website”.
Fast forward to this year, when I decided it was time to push for my web development career, my friend who was a web developer recommended LearningFuze to me. One thing about bootcamps that peaked my interest was that it would make you job-ready. What intimidated me the most about bootcamp was the full-time commitment it was asking of you. Joining a bootcamp meant sacrificing all of my time and effort and putting it into the bootcamp. I did not want to let go of competing and give up the time I spend training. However, the idea of starting my career and bridging the gap between what I want to accomplish in terms of side projects, was the reason I made the decision to join LearningFuze. Today, I am starting the third week of my Cohort.
Everyday I have been here has been amazing. I made new friends immediately and was able to relate to them about the struggles of trying to complete the project from RootJS. I’ve never felt so much anxiety about coding and never thought I’d be able to share that experience with anyone. I’ve met some friends who struggle with coding as much as I do, and being able to do that is what makes my journey at LearningFuze so surreal. 2 weeks in I have built 2 games, a guessing game and a memory match game, both which I’ve dedicated so much time in, more hours/times in a week than I have ever done in training for badminton. I am loving what I am doing here because everyday doesn’t feel like going to school or work. =======

About Noel

Noel is a free Bootstrap 3 theme created by Start Bootstrap. It can be yours right now, simply download the template on the preview page. The theme is open source, and you can use it for any purpose, personal or commercial.

This theme features stock photos by Gratisography along with a custom Google Maps skin courtesy of Snazzy Maps.

Noel includes full HTML, CSS, and custom JavaScript files along with LESS files for easy customization.
